Organizational Study with SWOT & PESTLE Analysis

MS Apparels, Tiruppur

Duration: 15 Days   •   Role: Researcher / Analyst

Overview

Conducted a focused organizational study at MS Apparels to evaluate structure, HR practices, workflow and operational processes. The study included stakeholder interviews, process observations, and document review.

Approach

  • Stakeholder interviews (management, supervisors, shop-floor staff)
  • Process observation (production flow, attendance, shift handovers)
  • Document review (policies, attendance logs, HR records)
  • SWOT and PESTLE analysis to identify internal and external factors

Key Findings

  • Strengths: Experienced supervisory staff, stable production processes in core lines.
  • Weaknesses: Limited formalized career development and retention programs.
  • Opportunities: Local skill-development partnerships and workplace safety improvements.
  • Threats: Supply chain delays and rising input costs impacting lead times.

SWOT Summary

Strengths
Skilled workforce, established client relationships.
Weaknesses
Informal HR processes, inconsistent onboarding.
Opportunities
Training programs, productivity initiatives.
Threats
Market volatility, regulatory changes.

PESTLE Highlights

  • Political: Local labour law enforcement trends.
  • Economic: Wage pressure and material cost fluctuations.
  • Social: Workforce demographics and migration patterns.
  • Technological: Opportunity for simple automation in packing and inventory.
  • Legal: Compliance requirements for contracts and safety.
  • Environmental: Waste reduction and sustainable sourcing potentials.

Recommendations

  1. Introduce a structured onboarding and induction checklist for new hires.
  2. Design a simple retention program: recognition + training calendar.
  3. Document SOPs for critical production and safety tasks.
  4. Pilot a partnership with a local training center to upskill entry-level workers.
